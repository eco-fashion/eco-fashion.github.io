What is Fast Fashion?

Fast fashion is a business model that focuses on mass-producing cheap, trendy clothing at high speeds. While it keeps costs low, the environmental impact is devastating.

Key Environmental Issues:

  • Water Consumption: It takes thousands of liters of water to produce a single cotton t-shirt.
  • Microplastics: Synthetic materials like polyester release microplastics into the ocean when washed.
  • Textile Waste: Millions of tons of clothing end up in landfills every year because items are designed to be thrown away quickly.
  • Carbon Emissions: The shipping and manufacturing processes contribute heavily to global warming.

To learn more about global environmental efforts, visit the United Nations Environment Programme .
